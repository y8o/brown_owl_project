Brown-Winged Owl Habitat Analysis

A GIS-based wildlife conservation project identifying nesting and foraging patterns of the fictional brown-winged owl species in Northern Michigan. The goal is to assist timber managers by identifying protected areas around sensitive habitats.

Key Findings

  • Owls strongly prefer oak and aspen cover types for nesting (91% of nests).
  • Average nest-to-foraging site distance is ~40 meters—proximity is key.
  • Oak has the largest absolute area lost (188.22 ha), but upland mixed has the highest relative loss (6.28%).
  • Overall, 286.34 ha or 5.04% of forested land is protected due to nesting buffers.
